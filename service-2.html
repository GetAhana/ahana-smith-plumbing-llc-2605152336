Water heaters

Water heater installation & replacement in Phoenix

Arizona's heat and hard water stress water heaters more than most regions. When yours fails or runs low, we size the right unit for your home and climate, install it safely, pressure-test connections, and handle disposal of the old tank.

Water heater specs for Arizona homes

  • Tank sizing based on household demand (40–80 gallons standard; 120+ for high-demand homes)
  • Tankless units (natural gas or electric) tested and vented per code for consistent hot water
  • Pressure relief and expansion tanks installed to meet Arizona code standards
  • One-year labor warranty plus manufacturer coverage on parts

We've installed and replaced over two hundred water heaters across Phoenix, Scottsdale, Tempe, and Mesa since 2012.

How we handle Water heaters

Our water heater replacement process is straightforward: assessment, approval, and professional installation.

  • We assess your needs — We check your current unit's age, condition, and capacity. We ask about your household's hot water use and any preference for tank versus tankless. Then we recommend the right size and type for your home.
  • You approve the install — We provide a written estimate with the unit cost, labor, venting or connection upgrades, and old-tank removal. No surprises—you know the total before we start.
  • We install and test — We handle safe venting, pressure-relief setup, and all connections per code. We pressure-test, flush sediment, walk you through the warranty, and haul away the old tank. Your new heater is ready to use.
When to call us

These signs often mean your water heater needs service or replacement soon.

  • Water takes longer to heat or runs cold quickly
  • Rust-colored water or metallic smell from hot taps
  • Leaking tank or water pooling around the base
  • Strange noises like rumbling or popping from the tank
  • Heater is over 10 years old and losing efficiency
